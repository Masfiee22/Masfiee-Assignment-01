Dr. Muhammad Yunus, a Bangladeshi social entrepreneur and Nobel Peace Prize laureate, is renowned for pioneering microfinance and founding Grameen Bank in 1983. Born in 1940, he revolutionized poverty alleviation by providing small, collateral-free loans to empower the poor, especially women.
